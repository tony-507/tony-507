Angle bisector

Here we consider the construction of angle bisector of an angle \(\angle AOB = \theta\). For simplicity we only consider the case of an acute angle.

  1. Fix a small radius \(r\) and draw an arc centered at O with radius \(r\). Denote the intersection of the arc with OA and OB by C and D respectively.
  2. Draw arcs centered at C and D respectively with radius \(r\). Denote the intersection of the two arcs other than O by E.
  3. OE is the desired angle bisector of \(\angle AOB\).

Let's see why it works.

A simple way to prove the result is to use polar coordinate system: We let \(O = (0,0), A = (1,0), B = (\cos\theta,\sin\theta)\) and \(r = 1\). Below is a figure for reference.

Note OA = OB = AC = BC = 1, so OABC is a parallelogram. It follows that $$ \angle COA = \frac{\pi - (\pi-\theta)}{2} = \frac{\theta}{2}$$ as desired.
