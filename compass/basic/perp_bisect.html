Perpendicular bisector

Here we explore the construction of perpendicular bisector of a straight line AB.

  1. Draw an arc centered at A with radius \(r\), where \(r\) is greater than half of the length of AB. Do the same thing for B.
  2. Denote the two intersections of the two arcs above by C and D. CD is the perpendicular bisector of AB.

The reason behind the construction is simple.

We can think of the two arcs as two circles centered at A and B respectively, with radius \(r\) . Then CD is a common chord to the two circles. By symmetry, AB bisects CD.

Since line joining mid-point of chord perpendicular chord, CD is perpendicular to AB.

Let E be the intersection of AB and CD. We claim \(\Delta AEC \cong \Delta BEC\). $$ \begin{align*} CE & = CE\\ AC & = BC\\ \angle AEC & = \angle BEC\end{align*} $$ Hence we have AE = BE as desired.
