Parallels

Here we consider the construction of parallel lines. Equivalently, we are talking about copying an angle. This can be done by intercept theorem. Suppose we are given two lines OCA and OB as follows, and we want to draw CD such that CD is parallel to OB.

We can copy the angle as follows.

  1. Draw an arc with length equal to OB centered at A. Denote the corresponding intersection of the arc with OA by E.
  2. Draw an arc with length equal to OB centered at C. Denote the intersection of the arc with OA by F.
  3. Draw an arc with length equal to BE centered at C. Denote the intersection of the arcs in (2) and (3) by D.
  4. CD is the desired line parallel to OA.

Let's see why the construction works.

The idea behind is congruent triangles. We claim \(\Delta OBE \cong \Delta CDF\), so it follows immediately that \(\angle BOE = \angle DCF\).

By our construction, $$\begin{align*} OB & = CD\\ OE & = CF\\ BE & = DF. \end{align*}$$

This finishes the proof.
