Means

Here we give a classic and efficient way to construct the four means of two numbers \(a\) and \(b\). The four means are:

  • Arithmetic mean: \(\displaystyle \frac{a+b}{2}\)
  • Geometric mean: \(\sqrt{ab}\)
  • Harmonic mean: \(\displaystyle \frac{2}{\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}}\)
  • Quadratic mean (RMS): \(\displaystyle \sqrt{\frac{a^2+b^2}{2}}\)

The construction is given here:

  1. Construct a straight line ACB, where AC has length \(a\) and BC has length \(b\).
  2. Draw a semi-circle with diameter AB. Let ED be the perpendicular bisector of AB, where E lies on the semi-circle.
  3. Let FC be the altitude of AC, where F lies on the semi-circle.
  4. Connect EC and FD. Let G be the perpendicular foot of C on FD.
  5. Now DE is the arithmetic mean, CF is the geometric mean, GF is the harmonic mean and CE is the quadratic mean.

Below proves our proposition.

The semi-circle has radius \(\displaystyle \frac{a+b}{2}\), so DE is the arithmetic mean.

For CF, note DF = \(\frac{a+b}{2}\) and CD = \(\frac{a-b}{2}\), so by Pythagoras theorem $$ CF = \sqrt{\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{a-b}{2}\right)^2} = \sqrt{ab}. $$

For GF, note \(\Delta FGC \sim \Delta FCD\), so $$ GF = CF \frac{CF}{DF} = \frac{2ab}{a+b} = \frac{2}{\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}}. $$

For CE, it follows from Pythagoras theorem that $$ CE = \sqrt{\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)^2+\left(\frac{a-b}{2}\right)^2} = \sqrt{\frac{a^2+b^2}{2}}. $$

In fact, this construction gives a visualisation of the HM-GM-AM-QM inequality for \(n=2\): $$ \frac{2}{\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}} \leq \sqrt{ab} \leq \frac{a+b}{2} \leq \sqrt{\frac{a^2+b^2}{2}}. $$
