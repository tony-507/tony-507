Converse of tangent property

Given two lines \(OA,OB\), can we find a circle with radius \(r\) and tangents \(OA,OB\)? To do this, we may first consider the case of having a circle.

We can easily see that \(OA\) has length \(r \cot x\) if the circle has radius \(r\). Then our construction can be done easily.

  1. Construct a segment of length \(r\cot x\) by constructing a right-angled triangle with angle \(x\) and a side with length \(r\).
  2. WLOG assume \(OA,OB\) has length \(r\cot x\). Draw perpendiculars of \(OA,OB\) at \(A,B\) respectively.
  3. The intersection of the two perpendiculars is the center of the desired circle.
