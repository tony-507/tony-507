Overview

In this section, we will explore whether the results of constructible numbers under arithmetic operations are still constructible. That is, if \(x\) and \(y\) are constructible, we want to determine the constructibility of \(x+y, x-y, xy, \frac{x}{y}\).

Eventually, we will see that constructibility is preserved under arithmetics, so all rational numbers are constructible.

We will prove these results by direct constructions.
