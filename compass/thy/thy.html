Constructibility of a point

Here we formally discuss the constructibility of a point. Familiarity of basic abstract algebra is assumed here. Let \(\mathcal{F}\) be the set of all constructible points. We assume \(0,1\in \mathcal{F}\). We also assume $$ x\in \mathcal{F} \Longrightarrow -x\in\mathcal{F}. $$

From previous discussions, we know that \(\mathcal{F}\) is closed under arithmetics. This implies that \(\mathcal{F}\) is a field. This immediately implies \(\mathbb{Q} \subset \mathcal{F}\).

However, we know that \(\sqrt{x}\in \mathcal{F}\) for \(x \in \mathcal{F}\), so \(\mathcal{F}\) must be a nontrivial extension of \(\mathcal{F}\). Here we show that all irrational constructible points are consecutive square roots of rationals.

When we perform straightedge and compass construction, we obtain new constructible point by intersecting straight lines, circles or a straight line and a circle. These corresponds to the three cases:

  1. $$ \begin{cases} ax+by+c = 0\\ dx+ey+f = 0\end{cases} $$
  2. $$ \begin{cases} (x-a)^2+(y-b)^2 = c^2\\ (x-d)^2+(y-e)^2 = f^2\end{cases} $$
  3. $$ \begin{cases} ax+by+c = 0\\ (x-d)^2+(y-e)^2 = f^2\end{cases} $$

When \(a,b,c,d,e,f \in \mathcal{F}\), the new constructible point obtained is either in \(\mathcal{F}\) or square root of element of \(\mathcal{F}\) by quadratic formula.

Now note a square root of a rational has a minimal polynomial of degree 2, so a consecutive square roots of a rational has a minimal polynomial of even degree. This classifies all constructible points.
