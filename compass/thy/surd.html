Surds

Here we consider construction of surds. That is, given a constructible number \(a\), how we can construct \(\sqrt{a}\).

The idea behind this construction is just a special case of geometric mean: the geometric mean of \(a\) and 1 is precisely \(\sqrt{a}\).

  1. Construct AB with length \(a\) and extend AB to AC with length \(a+1\).
  2. Draw a semi-circle with diameter AC.
  3. The perpendicular foot DB from the semi-circle to AC is the desired segment with length \(\sqrt{a}\).

The proof of the construction can be found in the section of means.
