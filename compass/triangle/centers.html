Four centers

There are four interesting centers of a triangle and we investigate them here.

  1. Orthocentre: Intersection of altitudes
  2. Centroid: Intersection of medians
  3. Circumcentre: Intersection of perpendicular bisectors
  4. Incentre: Intersection of angle bisectors

The constructions of the above geometrical objects have been discussed before. However, when it comes to finding all centers at once, some construction step becomes redundant. Let's see how the construction can be simplified.

For three centers other than incentre, we may begin by finding circumcentre as the construction steps of orthocentre and centroid becomes much less when we find perpendicular bisectors first. Finding two of the perpendicular bisectors give the circumcentre, and this can be done by drawing only three circles, one at each vertex with large enough radius.

After locating two of the mid-points of the triangle, we can find the centroid readily.

For orthocentre, we draw an extra circle on one of the mid-point, say M on OB, with radius MB. By angle in semi-circle we immediately obtain two altitudes.

For incentre, I don't think there is a shortcut to do so, we still need to construct two angle bisectors as before and find their intersection. However, when we want to find the inscribed circle, those mid-points may help.
