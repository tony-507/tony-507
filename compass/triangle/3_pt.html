Triangles from 3 points

Given three points, we can construct an isosceles triangle and an equilateral triangle through these points. This problem is a bit hard, so think before viewing the answer!

To construct isosceles triangle, we do the following.

  1. Connect AB and find its perpendicular bisector L. Draw a straight line through B,C that intersects L at D.
  2. Draw a line parallel to AB through C. Denote by F the point on the line that is equidistant from D to C.
  3. \(\Delta DFC\) is isosceles as desired.


We next move to the construction of equilateral triangle. This is a bit different from the above construction as the constraint is stronger. Again, think before seeing the solution!

Suppose AB has the shortest length among AB,BC,AC. C must be closer to one of A,B, and here we assume C is closer to B. The assumption that AB is the shortest ensures that the equilateral triangle can be formed. Now the triangle should take the following form.

We do the following.

  1. Draw a line that makes \(\frac{\pi}{3}\) to AB through C. Denote the intersection of the line with AB by D.
  2. If DC is shorter than DA, then the equilateral triangle has length DA. Otherwise, the equilateral triangle has length DC.
